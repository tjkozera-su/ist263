The Great Recession of 2007-2008

What was the 2007-2008 Financial Crisis?

Cause of The Great Recession


According to Forbes, the 2008-2009 was also called the Great Recession. This was the worst economic downturn since the Great Depression. Unemployment doubled to 10%, the housing market crashed and prices fell nearly 30%, and during its peak, the S&P 500 was down 57%

In the years leading up to the Great Recession, mortgages became easier to get with fewer standards in place to make sure they would be repaid. With more people looking to buy houses, there was a construction boom leading to a major price increase.

Failure of Banks and Bailouts


This phote is a graph of sharp increase of bankrupcy filings from January 2006-December 2008. The increase in bankrupcy filings was caused by the construction boom, federal funds rate increase, subprime mortgages, and credit default swaps.

There were nearly 200 banks that failed in 2008 and 2009. The costliest bank failures include: Bear Sterns which was acquired by JPMorgan for a price of $10 per share which is 94% below its 52-week high, and Lehman Brothers with over 639 billion in assets when they filed for bankruptcy.
